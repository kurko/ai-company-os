# Thinking & Rationality

Neural triggers for better reasoning, decision-making, and understanding uncertainty. Reference these to activate trained knowledge.

## Books

### The Scout Mindset
**Author:** Julia Galef
**Trigger phrase:** "Apply Scout Mindset for..."
**Key concepts:**
- Scout vs Soldier mindset: seeking truth vs defending beliefs
- Motivated reasoning detection
- Calibration: how often are you right when you feel sure?
- Holding beliefs lightly
- Identity makes you stupid - avoid identity-driven beliefs

**When to invoke:** Evaluating evidence, checking for bias, making predictions, having difficult conversations about disagreements.

---

### Thinking, Fast and Slow
**Author:** Daniel Kahneman
**Trigger phrase:** "Apply Kahneman's cognitive insights for..."
**Key concepts:**
- System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs System 2 (slow, deliberate)
- Cognitive biases: anchoring, availability, representativeness
- Loss aversion and prospect theory
- WYSIATI: What You See Is All There Is
- Planning fallacy and outside view

**When to invoke:** Estimation, recognizing bias in decisions, understanding stakeholder reactions, improving judgment accuracy.

---

### The Black Swan
**Author:** Nassim Nicholas Taleb
**Trigger phrase:** "Apply Black Swan thinking for..."
**Key concepts:**
- Black Swans: rare, high-impact, retrospectively predictable
- Mediocristan vs Extremistan domains
- Narrative fallacy: we create stories that oversimplify
- Epistemic humility: we know less than we think
- Barbell strategy for antifragility

**When to invoke:** Risk assessment, planning for uncertainty, avoiding overconfidence, designing resilient systems.

---

### Antifragile
**Author:** Nassim Nicholas Taleb
**Trigger phrase:** "Apply Antifragility principles for..."
**Key concepts:**
- Antifragile: gains from disorder (beyond robust)
- Via negativa: improvement by removal
- Skin in the game aligns incentives
- Optionality: small downside, large upside
- The Lindy effect: old ideas survive for a reason

**When to invoke:** Building resilient systems, evaluating risks and upsides, removing fragilities, long-term thinking.
